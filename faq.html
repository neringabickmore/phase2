Our Most Frequently Asked Questions

Having a blow-dry after your haircut or colour service is an essential step in achieving the best results and overall experience.
Here are some reasons why:

  • Enhanced Styling: A blow-dry allows our stylists to shape and style your hair to perfection. Whether you prefer sleek and straight, voluminous waves, or a bouncy blowout, a professional blow-dry ensures your haircut or colour looks its absolute best.
  • Showcasing Your Colour: After a colour service, a blow-dry helps to reveal the true vibrancy and depth of your new shade. The heat from the blow dryer activates the shine and enhances the overall appearance of the colour, allowing you to see the full effect of your service.
  • Sealing in Moisture: Blow-drying helps to seal the hair cuticles, locking in moisture and colour. This is especially important after colouring, as it can help prevent fading and keep your hair looking fresh and vibrant for longer.
  • Long-Lasting Results: A professional blow-dry ensures that your style is set and lasts longer. It helps to create a smooth finish and provides added hold, so you can enjoy your new look without worrying about frizz or drooping.
  • Boosting Confidence: Leaving the salon with a beautifully styled blow-dry not only enhances your overall look but also boosts your confidence. It’s the perfect way to showcase your new haircut or colour and makes you feel like a million dollars.

At Phase 2.0 Hair Lounge, we believe that a blow-dry is not just an add-on service but a vital part of the haircut and colour experience. Our skilled stylists are dedicated to ensuring you leave the salon feeling and looking your best.

Balayage is a freehand hair coloring technique where highlights are painted onto the hair to create a natural, sun-kissed look with softer regrowth lines compared to traditional highlights. At Phase 2.0 Hair Lounge, our skilled Phase 2.0 colourists specialize in this technique to give you a personalized result.
To care for your balayage, use a sulfate-free, color-safe shampoo and conditioner to protect the color and prevent fading.
Deep condition regularly to keep your hair hydrated and maintain the shine of your balayage.
Limit heat styling and always use a heat protectant to avoid damage that can dull the color.
Avoid over-washing your hair—try washing 2-3 times a week to keep your balayage vibrant for longer.
Schedule touch-ups with our Phase 2.0 colourists every 8-12 weeks to keep your balayage looking fresh and blended.

Toning is the key to keeping your colour looking fresh, vibrant, and free of unwanted brassy or yellow tones. Whether you've had balayage, highlights, or a full blonde transformation, a toner is essential immediately after your colour service to perfect the shade and achieve the desired tone. Our Phase 2.0 colourists expertly apply toners to balance undertones, neutralise warmth, and leave you with a flawless finish.
In addition to the initial application, toning is recommended around 6 weeks after your colour service. This refreshes and revives your colour without needing a full touch-up, prolonging the life of your balayage, highlights, or platinum blonde. Toners not only adjust colour but also add extra shine, leaving your hair looking healthy and glossy.
To keep your shade looking its best, we suggest regular toning sessions every 4-6 weeks, along with a personalised at-home care routine using toning shampoos or conditioners. This helps maintain your colour’s vibrancy between appointments.

Scandi Blonde is the dreamy ultra-light, icy blonde look. This striking, high-lift blonde delivers a cool, platinum finish that’s clean, bright, and effortlessly chic. It is ideal for clients looking for a bold transformation.
Scandi Blonde requires expert precision and care, and our Phase 2.0 colourists specialize in achieving that flawless, even blonde.
The process involves lifting the hair to its palest possible shade while maintaining hair health, followed by toning to eliminate any warmth or brassiness, leaving you with that signature cool-toned blonde.
The aftercare is key: regular toning treatments every 6-8 weeks will keep your blonde icy and fresh, while a strong moisturizing and strengthening regimen will help protect your hair from the lightening process.

When it comes to achieving a flawless hair colour, understanding the nuances between techniques can make all the difference. At Phase 2.0 Hair Lounge, we offer various root techniques to enhance your colour service. Here’s a breakdown of three popular methods: root tap, root melt, and root drag.

  • Root Tap: This technique involves applying a slightly darker shade or a colour that matches your natural roots at the base of the hair. The goal of a root tap is to create a seamless transition between your natural colour and your highlights or balayage. It softens the contrast, making regrowth less noticeable and allowing for a more blended, natural look.
  • Root Melt: A root melt combines different shades to create a soft, blended effect at the roots. This technique is perfect for clients who want a subtle transition from their natural colour to lighter ends. The stylist uses a darker shade at the roots and gradually blends it into a lighter hue, creating a soft ombre effect. This approach adds depth and dimension while maintaining a harmonious appearance.
  • Root Drag: Root drag is a technique where the colour is 'dragged' down from the roots for a more elongated, diffused look. This method works well for creating a lived-in colour effect, where the roots are slightly darker and gradually blend into lighter mid-lengths and ends. Root drag is ideal for those who prefer a softer, more natural grow-out with less maintenance.

A money piece refers to a section of hair, typically located at the front or around the face, that is highlighted or coloured in a way that draws attention to the face. This technique enhances the overall look and can create a bright, youthful appearance.
Here are some key points about the money piece:

  • Placement: The money piece is usually placed around the face, often in the bangs or framing sections, to highlight and brighten the features.
  • Contrast: It is often a lighter shade than the rest of the hair, creating a striking contrast. This can be achieved through techniques like balayage, foiling, or even fashion colours.
  • Versatility: A money piece can be incorporated into various hairstyles and colour combinations, making it a versatile option for anyone looking to refresh their look.
  • Maintenance: Depending on the desired effect, the money piece may require regular touch-ups to maintain brightness and contrast, but it can also grow out gracefully, making it a low-maintenance option for some.
  • Styling: The money piece can be styled in various ways, whether left natural, curled, or straightened, making it a fun addition to your hair colour.

Heat styling is often unavoidable, but that doesn't mean you can't protect your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Always apply a heat protective heat spray before using any heat styling tools to minimize damage.
  • Limit heat exposure by using tools on the lowest effective heat setting and avoid daily use of heat styling tools.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture and repair damage.
  • Use a treatment mask designed for heat-damaged hair once a week.
  • Invest in high-quality heat styling tools that distribute heat evenly and reduce hot spots, which can cause excessive damage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to hydrate and smooth your hair, helping it look shinier and healthier.
  • Air dry whenever possible to give your hair a break from heat.
  • Gettting regular trims every 6-8 weeks to remove split ends and prevent further breakage.

Curly hair requires specialized care to maintain its natural bounce and minimize frizz. Here is our advice how you can care for your curly hair:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo to clean your curls without stripping natural oils, and deep condition regularly to keep hair soft and moisturized.
  • Detangle gently with a wide-tooth comb or fingers while your hair is wet and conditioned to reduce breakage.
  • Apply leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair for moisture retention and curl definition.
  • Air dry or use a diffuser on low heat to reduce frizz, and always use a heat protective spray when heat styling.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage, and trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy ends.
  • Refresh your curls between washes with a curl-refreshing spray or a mix of water and conditioner to keep them looking fresh.

The key to maintaining lustrous and healthy hair post-color treatment is intensive care.

  • Use sulfate-free, color-safe shampoo to gently cleanse your hair without stripping away the color or natural oils.
  • Deep condition weekly to restore moisture and keep your hair soft and shiny. Look for masks specifically designed for color-treated hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or serum to help lock in moisture and protect your hair from damage and frizz.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair. Wash 2-3 times a week to preserve your hair color and keep it smooth.
  • imit heat styling to avoid further damage. If using heat, always apply a heat protective spray to shield your hair.
  • Rinse with cool water to seal the hair cuticle and enhance shine after every wash.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and maintain smooth, healthy-looking hair.

Thinning hair can be a concern for many, but there are effective ways to manage it and promote healthier growth.

  • Use a volumizing or thickening shampoo to give your hair a fuller appearance while strengthening it.
  • Avoid over-washing and use gentle, sulfate-free products to prevent further damage to thinning hair.
  • Incorporate scalp massages to improve blood circulation, which can promote hair growth.
  • Take supplements like biotin, collagen, or vitamins specifically designed for hair health, such as Glowwa Hair Food (available at Phase 2.0 Hair Lounge).
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can put strain on your scalp and cause hair loss, like ponytails or braids.
  • Limit heat styling and chemical treatments that can further weaken thinning hair.

Taming frizz can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve smooth, manageable hair.

  • Use sulfate-free, anti-frizz shampoo and conditioner to retain moisture and smooth your hair, followed by a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum on damp hair to seal the cuticle.
  • Dry your hair with a microfiber towel or t-shirt to reduce friction, and rinse with cool water after conditioning to close the hair cuticle and lock in smoothness.
  • Limit heat styling and always use a heat protective spray to avoid damage that leads to frizz.
  • Avoid brushing dry hair—use a wide-tooth comb on wet hair to detangle without causing frizz.
  • Deep condition weekly with a mask designed to fight frizz and use humidity-resistant products to keep frizz at bay throughout the day.
